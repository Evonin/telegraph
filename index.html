Telegraph


Have you ever wanted to talk to people across the country? Well for $2000 and $0.74/word, you can with the new invention called the Telegraph! This new system was created by Samuel Morse and it uses electricity to create signals, which travel between long distances. For this electricity to travel, the telegraph sends short pulses through a series of wires. Beside different roads and buildings, Morse is planning to add something called a telephone line, this wire will run throughout the country and sends each pulse of electricity to the desired destination.

Who made this?

The telegraph was made by inventor, Samuel Morse. He is a professor of art at New York University and likes to invent new things for solving different problems. Before this he was a painter and always wanted to paint paintings at the US capital but hasn't been picked to attend.
