Telegraph


Have you ever wanted to talk to people across the country? Well for $2000 and $0.74/word, you can with the new invention called the Telegraph! This new invention uses electricity to create signals which travel between long distances. These signals can be translated into a language called morse code. For these signals to travel, the telegraph sends them through a series of wires. These wires are hung on the side of roads, which act like runways for your coordinated message.

Who made this?

The telegraph was made by inventor, Samuel Morse. He is a professor of art at New York University and likes to invent new things for solving different problems. Before this he was a painter and always wanted to paint paintings at the US capital but hasn't been picked to attend.
